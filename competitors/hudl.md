# Hudl Competitor Report

## Company overview

| Field | Research finding |
| --- | --- |
| Category | Sports video, data, performance analysis, recruiting, automated capture, and team operations technology |
| Company | Agile Sports Technologies, Inc. dba Hudl |
| Primary customers | Teams, schools, clubs, colleges, professional organizations, coaches, analysts, athletes, and recruiters. |
| Positioning | Hudl says it helps teams capture, organize, analyze, and learn from film and data. |
| Relevance to Nine One | Benchmark for coach/team video workflows, AI tagging, performance analysis, smart cameras, and recruiting/highlight tools. |

## Target users

| Segment | Needs |
| --- | --- |
| Coaches and analysts | Capture video, review film, tag plays, create playlists, analyze stats, scout opponents, and share feedback. |
| Athletes | Study video, view highlights, receive assignments, and create recruiting material. |
| Clubs/schools | Manage multiple teams, storage, streaming, ticketing/operations, and recruiting workflows. |
| Elite/pro organizations | Advanced performance analysis, custom data products, scouting, and workflow integrations. |

## Core features

| Area | Features |
| --- | --- |
| Video platform | Upload, store, share, review, clip, annotate, and organize team video. |
| Analysis | Hudl Assist, Sportscode, Insight, Studio, Hudl IQ, Instat, Volleymetrics, Statsbomb, and other analysis products. |
| Automated capture | Hudl Focus Indoor/Outdoor/Flex/Point smart cameras for automated capture, livestream, and upload. |
| Recruiting/scouting | Recruit, Wyscout, Wyscout Data, Instat, FastRecruit, highlight and athlete profile workflows. |
| Team operations | Sports organization management, registration, messaging, scheduling, communication tools. |

## UX flow

1. Team captures or uploads game/practice video, often via Hudl camera or device.
2. Video is organized, tagged, and analyzed by software, AI, or Hudl Assist analysts depending on package.
3. Coaches create clips, playlists, comments, telestration, reports, and assignments.
4. Athletes review film, highlights, playbooks, and feedback.
5. Recruiters/scouts access athlete video and data where applicable.

## Monetization

| Revenue stream | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Team subscriptions | Hudl public high-school pricing lists Silver+ at $1,500/program/year, Gold+ at $2,500/program/year, Platinum+ at $4,000/program/year. |
| Club subscriptions | Club football pricing lists Bronze at $400/team/year, Silver at $1,000/team/year, Gold at $1,600/team/year. |
| Custom packages | Professional pricing says every organization is different and packages are built with the sales team. |
| Hardware/software bundles | Automated capture products and Focus Flex football packages start from $2,000 USD on one Hudl page. |
| Add-on services | Hudl Assist and other analysis/reporting products are part of package comparisons; exact add-on pricing varies or is custom. |

## Strengths

| Strength | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Deep video workflow maturity | Hudl is built around coach review, tagging, playlists, and team learning. |
| Broad product ecosystem | Capture, video, analytics, recruiting, operations, and fan engagement are connected. |
| Multi-level market coverage | Serves youth, club, high school, college, and professional markets. |
| Automated capture hardware | Removes filming burden for teams and enables consistent video libraries. |
| Recruiting network | Athlete highlights and scouting products add career-value use cases. |

## Weaknesses

| Weakness | Evidence / rationale |
| --- | --- |
| Heavy team/coach orientation | Less suited to casual individual padel players wanting quick booking and improvement. |
| Public pricing can be high for small venues | Published packages can exceed early-stage club software budgets. |
| Mobile complaints | Google Play reviews complain about Android crashes, lag, login issues, upload limitations, and playback controls. |
| Complexity | Inference: broad enterprise ecosystem can be overkill for recreational padel clubs. |
| Not padel-specific | No public padel-specific player matchmaking/booking product was found. |

## User complaints

| Theme | Reported complaint |
| --- | --- |
| Android usability | Google Play review says Android experience is laggy/buggy and logs users out. |
| Playback controls | Google Play and Chrome Stats reviews mention missing fast-forward/rewind or timeline controls. |
| Upload constraints | Google Play review complained about not being able to record/upload over mobile data. |
| Capture framing | App Store snippet complained that indoor volleyball recording was too zoomed in to see the whole court. |
| Streaming quality | Reddit users discuss streaming-quality concerns; anecdotal. |

## Strategic observations

| Observation | Implication for Nine One |
| --- | --- |
| Hudl owns the serious coach/team video workflow. | Nine One should adapt the best parts for recreational padel: fast highlights, simple coach notes, and lightweight review. |
| Automated capture is a durable advantage. | Nine One's smart venue strategy should include reliable court cameras or phone-mount alternatives. |
| Video alone is not enough for padel venues. | Pair video with booking, community, lessons, and retention analytics. |
| Enterprise complexity leaves a consumer/club gap. | Nine One can be simpler, padel-native, and more affordable for small-to-mid venues. |

## Opportunity gap for Nine One

| Gap | Product opportunity |
| --- | --- |
| Hudl is not recreational padel-native | Create padel session recaps, partner dynamics, tactical clips, and skill-level tracking. |
| Video workflows are too coach-heavy | Offer player-first highlights and coach review only when needed. |
| No booking/venue marketplace | Connect analysis to court booking, open matches, clinics, and memberships. |
| Small club affordability | Provide modular smart-coaching tools that do not require enterprise sports budgets. |
