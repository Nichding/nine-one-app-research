# Playtomic Competitor Report

## Company overview

| Field | Research finding |
| --- | --- |
| Category | Racket-sports booking marketplace and club-management platform |
| Primary sports | Padel, tennis, pickleball, and other racket sports |
| Positioning | Playtomic describes itself as "the world's leading App for racket sport players and clubs." |
| Geographic footprint | Public reporting says Playtomic operates in 65 countries; Playtomic's 2024 global padel report press material said 52 countries. Treat exact current country count as variable. |
| Scale | Public reporting from Cadena SER cited 1.5M monthly active players and about 6,000 clubs, but this is third-party reporting, not an audited company disclosure. |

## Target users

| Segment | Needs |
| --- | --- |
| Players | Find nearby courts, join open matches, invite friends, chat, pay, track level, and discover clubs. |
| Clubs and venue chains | Court scheduling, payments, reports, customer management, events, classes, leagues, POS, memberships, and high court utilization. |
| Coaches and academies | Private classes, courses, coach availability, and bookable academy programs. |

## Core features

| Area | Features |
| --- | --- |
| Booking | Court search, club profiles, private bookings, open matches, activities, payments, and service-fee handling. |
| Community | Player profiles, player level system, match discovery, invitations, match chat, and post-match results. |
| Club management | Scheduling, blocking, payments, reports, tournaments, customer records, classes, POS, memberships, invoices, leagues, API integrations, campaigns, and multi-entity support depending on plan. |
| Coaching | Playtomic Academy supports classes and courses connected to coach and court availability. |

## UX flow

1. Player opens the app and searches by club, sport, location, or available match.
2. Player chooses either a private court booking or an open match.
3. For open matches, the player sees level information and available slots, joins, pays, and can use match chat.
4. After play, results can feed profile and level mechanics.
5. Club-side UX focuses on managing schedules, court inventory, payments, classes, reports, and customer behavior from Playtomic Manager.

## Monetization

| Revenue stream | Evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Club SaaS plans | Playtomic lists Standard, Professional, Champion, and Master plans; public page does not expose numeric plan prices. |
| Player/service fees | Playtomic help states a service fee can be charged for reservations, open matches, tournaments, and activities, varying by country and currency. |
| Payment/transaction commission | A Playtomic self-invoicing PDF references "Playtomic Commission" on payments made through Playtomic. Exact current rates vary and are Unknown. |
| Premium/subscription | User reviews mention Premium; official current premium packaging/pricing was not verified in this research. |

## Strengths

| Strength | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Strong two-sided network | Players want clubs where there are opponents; clubs want demand and utilization. |
| Racket-specific workflows | Open matches, levels, chat, classes, and club management are purpose-built for racket sports. |
| Broad club operations surface | Combines booking, payments, reporting, courses, leagues, POS, and API options. |
| Community mechanics | Leveling and open matches create repeat engagement beyond simple reservations. |

## Weaknesses

| Weakness | Evidence / rationale |
| --- | --- |
| Fee sensitivity | Help documentation confirms service fees; community threads and reviews complain about fees and confusion. |
| Customer support friction | Trustpilot reviews and App Store snippets mention chatbot/support issues. |
| Level/rating trust issues | User reviews complain about level assessments, rating algorithm behavior, and match imbalance. |
| Player web experience gap | Reddit users ask why there is no workable player-centric web app. |
| Platform dependency risk for clubs | Inference: clubs relying heavily on Playtomic may have limited ownership of player relationship and community data. |

## User complaints

| Theme | Reported complaint |
| --- | --- |
| Support | "Support center is not working" and chatbot issues appear in public reviews. |
| Leveling | Reviews mention rating drops, restrictive level controls, and paid coach-level assessment dissatisfaction. |
| Fees | Users discuss service fees, fee confusion, and historical tests around paid external match/score features. |
| UX regressions | Reddit users complain about changes to public/private match creation and web/event visibility. |
| Trust and integrity | Reddit discussions mention fake accounts or clubs using fake-looking profiles; this is user-reported and not independently verified. |

## Strategic observations

| Observation | Implication for Nine One |
| --- | --- |
| Playtomic is strongest where marketplace liquidity matters. | A new entrant should avoid competing only on booking inventory unless it can seed local density. |
| The level system is valuable but trust-sensitive. | Nine One can differentiate with transparent skill calibration, match quality explanations, and coach/video-assisted validation. |
| Clubs may want community growth but dislike platform lock-in. | Offer stronger first-party club data ownership, CRM exportability, and branded community tools. |
| Support and fee transparency are recurring pain points. | Nine One should make pricing, cancellation rules, and human support pathways explicit. |

## Opportunity gap for Nine One

| Gap | Product opportunity |
| --- | --- |
| Booking plus coaching is fragmented | Combine court booking, match formation, smart coaching, session recap, and improvement plan in one flow. |
| Level systems lack explainability | Use transparent confidence scores, recent-performance weighting, coach validation, and optional video evidence. |
| Clubs need smarter utilization tools | Add demand forecasting, flexible off-peak offers, loyalty segments, and campaign automation. |
| Players still use WhatsApp | Build club-approved groups, trusted player availability, and structured match invites that reduce external coordination. |
| Trust and safety | Add verified profiles, attendance reliability, no-show history, and privacy-aware discovery controls. |
