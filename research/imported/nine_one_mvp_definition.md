# Nine One MVP Definition

## Strategic thesis

Nine One's MVP should be:

**A padel-native match-quality and improvement platform for partner venues.**

The MVP should help players find better matches, help venues fill courts with reliable players, and turn every completed match into structured data that improves future recommendations.

Booking is required, but it should not be the product's strategic center. Playtomic, MATCHi, CourtReserve, and Padel Mates already cover booking and venue operations. Nine One should use booking as the capture point for higher-value data: who played, reliability, skill confidence, match balance, player intent, and improvement needs.

## Product promise

| Audience | Promise |
| --- | --- |
| Players | "Find reliable players at your level and know what to improve after every match." |
| Venues | "Fill more courts with better-matched players and build a community your club owns." |
| Coaches | "Turn real match moments into lessons, clinics, and measurable player progression." |

## MVP wedge

| Layer | MVP decision | Why it is strategically leveraged |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Venue entry point | Launch with 3-5 partner padel venues in one city or region | Network effects require local density, not broad but thin coverage. |
| Core transaction | Open match booking, not full club ERP | Open matches create player-player liquidity and data faster than private bookings alone. |
| Differentiator | Match quality score and skill confidence | Moves beyond commodity booking into trust and recommendation quality. |
| Retention loop | Post-match recap with next action | Creates a Strava-like progress habit tied to padel. |
| Data moat | Structured match, reliability, skill, and coaching data | Improves recommendations over time and becomes hard to replicate locally. |

## Core MVP modules

| Module | Include in MVP | Exclude from MVP |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Player profile | Name, photo, home venue, preferred side, availability, self-rated level, coach-validated level, reliability score, match history | Full social feed, lifestyle profile, broad fitness tracking |
| Open match system | Create/join match, level range, payment state, cancellation rules, slot expiry, waitlist, match chat | Full tournament engine, complex leagues, marketplace across many regions |
| Venue console | Court inventory, open-match templates, schedule visibility, fill-rate dashboard, player reliability view, manual override | POS, inventory, payroll, accounting, full membership CRM |
| Post-match recap | Result, attendance, balance rating, player feedback, level confidence update, next recommended match/clinic | Full AI video breakdown, automated line calling, advanced biometric analytics |
| Coach layer | Coach validation, simple notes, recommended clinic/lesson, optional tag of improvement area | Full Hudl-style telestration and video library |

## MVP north-star metric

| Metric | Definition | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Quality completed matches | Completed open matches with all players paid/confirmed, no no-shows, and a positive balance rating | Captures marketplace liquidity, trust, venue value, and player satisfaction in one metric. |

## Supporting metrics

| Metric | Target signal |
| --- | --- |
| Open match fill rate | Measures whether Nine One creates liquidity. |
| Repeat player rate within 30 days | Measures player retention. |
| Match balance score | Measures differentiation versus generic booking. |
| No-show / late cancellation rate | Measures reliability system value. |
| Coach/clinic conversion after recap | Measures monetizable improvement loop. |
| Off-peak court utilization | Measures venue ROI. |

## Strategic non-goals

| Non-goal | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Become a full club-management suite immediately | CourtReserve and Playtomic already cover deep operations; this would dilute a small team. |
| Build a broad marketplace before density | Thin inventory produces weak player experience and poor network effects. |
| Launch with full AI video analysis | SwingVision and Hudl show this is valuable but complex; Nine One first needs structured match data and capture workflows. |
| Compete on lowest booking fee | Fee competition is easy to copy and weakens margins. |
| Build generic sports community features | Strava-style mechanics should be adapted to padel match progress, not copied as a broad feed. |
